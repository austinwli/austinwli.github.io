## Background and goals
Bayesian Improved Surname Geocoding (BISG) is a method for proxying race and ethnicity. The method was developed and detailed by RAND in 2008 to study health coverage to get a general sense of racial or ethnic composition of an area. BISG uses Bayesian statistics to construct a probability of assignment to race or ethnic group based on demographic information associated with surname, and then updates the probabilities using the demographic characteristics associated with place of residence. In a [2016 paper](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5158280/), RAND claims that the model is 90-96% accurate for the four largest racial/ethnic groups.

BISG is widely accepted as the standard for estimating race and ethnicity in fair lending analysis. Moreover, this method is implemented by lenders across the country and has since been widely accepted as the de facto standard for estimating race and ethnicity in fair lending analysis.

We sought to improve the process with machine learning and supplementary data, because as is BISG stands in the way of addressing racial disparity in credit today.

BISG assumes the following:
- It uses limited data and elementary data science
- It relies on surnames
- Assumes we live in segregated neighborhoods

To circumvent these shortcomings, we did the following:
- Developed a geographic-agnostic national model that can generalize to any state, starting with Florida and North Carolina voter registration data. We added ACS and Census data to provide additional demographic and location-specific context
- These additional parameters make the model more generalizable and does not assume neighborhood segregation
- Developed a high-accuracy low-cost geocoding method to convert addresses to coordinates for model-building

The resulting model performed better for the four largest racial/ethnic groups on a sample of 70000 samples from NOrth Carolina.
